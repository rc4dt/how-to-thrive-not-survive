Hello my dear blog readers welcome to my skin care journey part 2. Today I'm going to delve into my journey using contraceptives for my acne and my experience at the dermatologists.

In December 2022, I visited the dermatologist. I waited for this appointment for months and was eagerly awaiting for an answer. The options given to me were either Acutane or birth control pills. I was 14 years old at the time. My mother was reluctant to let me take birth control, though I was persistent as it seemed to have less side effects (and also made me feel grown). The appointment lasted for a total of 7 mins. At the time I didn't question it but now it really feels ridiculous that a medicine that affected my life so much was carelessly prescribed. It really put into perspective the reality of our healthcare system.

For the first few months I had constant nausea, developed brain fog and experienced many mood alterations. As I persisted on this medicine my appetite began to grow and I started to put on some weight. My body changed quickly and my breasts and hips became fuller. I also developed constant fatigue. Although my skin did begin to see improvement. I thought my symptoms would subside but they persisted to the following year.

In the second half of grade 10, after my volleyball season I gained several pounds and saw immense changes in my mood. I began feeling extremely unmotivated and saw an overwhelming decrease in my social battery. Often my routine would consist of going to school, sleeping, doom scrolling and sleeping late at night. I found it hard to focus in class and while studying due to brain fog. Although my most detrimental symptoms was the extreme emotional switches. During my luteal phase I would become extremely irritable and anxious and in turn strained many of my relationships.

During my third year on the pill, I got used to many of the symptoms but they still persisted and affected my day to day life. I finally made the decision in March 2024 to get off my medication to pursue a more holistic lifestyle. I will continue my journey in part 3!
